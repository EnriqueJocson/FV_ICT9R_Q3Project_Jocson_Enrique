TAYLOR SWIFT

Taylor Alison Swift is an American singer and songwriter. She was born in December 13, 1989 in West Reading, Pennsylvania, U.S.A. and her parents are Andrea Swift and Scott Kingsley Swift. She only has one sibling which is her brother Austin Swift.

She has captivated audiences with her heartfelt lyrics and catchy melodies, solidifying herself as one of the most influential artists in contemporary music.

Taylor's most famous songs include You Belong With Me, Love Story, I Knew You We're Trouble, Shake It Off, Blank Space, Look What You Made Me Do, Cruel Summer, and Anti-Hero.

She currently has 10 albums: Taylor Swift (Debut Album), Fearless, Speak Now, Red, 1989, Reputation, Lover, Folklore, Evermore, and Midnights. She will soon release her 11th album titled "The Tortured Poets Department" on April 19, 2024 which was announced in the 2024 Grammy's.

In 2018 Swift left Big Machine and signed with Republic Records and Universal Music Group. The following year, her first six albums (Debut, Fearless, Speak Now, Red, 1989, and Reputation), was sold to Scooter Braun. She had no choice but to re-record the songs in those albums. Currently, her re-recorded albums are Fearless (Taylor's Version), Speak Now (Taylor's Version), Red (Taylor's Version), and 1989 (Taylor's Version). Each re-recorded album also contains songs From The Vault which aren't in the original albums.

She is also found in movies such as Taylor Swift: The Eras Tour (2023 Film), Cats (2019 Film) and The Lorax (2012 Film)

In 2024 she made history when she won the Grammy Award for album of the year for Midnights (2022), becoming the first artist to win in that category four times.

She is now considered as one of the most famous singers and songwriters in the world. Taylor Swift's fanbase is called "Swifties".
